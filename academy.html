Module 01: Core Vulnerabilities

OWASP Top 10: Architectural Analysis

The OWASP Top 10 serves as the definitive framework for modern web security. It is not merely a list of bugs, but a classification of systemic architectural failures.

A01:2021-Broken Access Control

Access control enforces policy such that users cannot act outside of their intended permissions. When these controls fail, it typically leads to unauthorized information disclosure, modification, or destruction of data.

A03:2021-Injection Vectors

Injection flaws occur when untrusted data is sent to an interpreter as part of a command or query. This encompasses SQL, NoSQL, OS, and LDAP injection.

Module 02: Cryptographic Standards

Advanced Cryptographic Protocols

Cryptography is the mathematical foundation of digital trust. Modern systems must balance cryptographic strength with operational performance.

Symmetric Encryption: AES-256-GCM

The Advanced Encryption Standard (AES) with a 256-bit key is the current gold standard. The GCM (Galois/Counter Mode) provides both confidentiality and Authenticated Encryption.

Elliptic Curve Cryptography (ECC)

For data-in-transit (TLS), ECC offers significantly higher security per bit than RSA, leading to faster handshakes and reduced CPU overhead.

Case Study: Intelligence Analysis

The 2025 'Hydra' Breach: A Post-Mortem

The Hydra breach stands as a landmark case in Cloud Infrastructure Security. The root cause was a failure of the Defense in Depth strategy.

Incident Vector: SSRF to IAM

The attacker leveraged a Server-Side Request Forgery (SSRF) vulnerability in a PDF-rendering microservice to retrieve temporary IAM credentials from the metadata service.

The Failure of Privilege:

The microservice's IAM role possessed 'Administrator' permissions. Enforcing the Principle of Least Privilege would have contained the blast radius.
